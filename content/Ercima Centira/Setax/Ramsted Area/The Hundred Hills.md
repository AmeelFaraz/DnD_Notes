The mountain range at the southern part of the [[Ramsted]] area. 

---
## Lore

#### Description
This range, while low in overall height, are prominent as the only major source of elevation change in the area. [[The Hundred Hills]] are an important part of the [[Ramsted]] economy, as the city depends on the various metals the [[Hundred Hills Mine]] produces for several day-to -day products as well as things to trade with. It is also the sole way several factions and organizations in the city create or upkeep their weapons. 

#### Other
[[The Hundred Hills]] were formed over the remains of an Ancient Tarrasque, the mythical monster known as [[The Wrath]]. the land in particular is important as the scar in the earth that [[The Wrath]] finally settled down to rest in forever was once an area blessed by  a dao. this, combined with the settlement of the the [[Azers]] and the creation of the [[Azer Holy Forge]], enabled the mine in the hundred hills to produce multiple different types of metals.

## Conflicts/Quests

The mine in the [[The Hundred Hills]] has stopped sending its shipments of metal into the city. this has caused considerable problems, as control over the flow of metals from the [[Hundred Hills Mine]]is a major point of contention in the city. 

- The [[Grand Church of Science]] seeks to investigate the cause of the suspended metal shipments. while they are not defenseless without the metals due to shipments from other locations of The [[Grand Church of Science]], however they are interested in keeping the resources out of the other factions hands.

- The [[Wyrdkin]] are interested in getting the flow of metals going again, as they require the metal in order to arm their forces for [[Immasi's uprising]]. [[Immasi]] is also suspicious of the fact that his teams have not been able to successfully intercept [[Grand Church of Science]] weapon shipments. Still, they know of some rumors regarding the [[The Hundred Hills]], and furnish the party with maps and rumors about shaking earth and hooded figures. 

- The current blacksmith in [[Ramsted]]  wants to know why the flow of metals has stopped, and is desperate to get it going again due to his livelihood being at stake. He promises the services of his master, the master blacksmith [[Bram Ironskin]], as payment to solve the issue.

- The village surrounding the entrance to the [[Hundred Hills Mine]] seems to be under a strange spell. There are Hill Trolls wandering around the village, seemingly doing tasks, yet no one acknowledges them. When asked about it, the villagers act as though they forget what was said to them.
