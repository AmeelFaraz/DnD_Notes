## Pope of the Grand Church of Science 

The head of the church, he oversees everything about the church. A long and storied position, the nature and personality of the pope has widespread ramifications on its actions and operations

## High Clergy 
### Cardinal of the Grand Church of Science

A group of the most important members of the church. The pope’s successor is chosen from amongst the cardinals by the previous pope. This leads to many cardinals curry for favor with the pope rather than doing their administrative duties over their designated regions.
Cardinals can be established in the Central Grand Church or in any particular church within their region.%% Recently, more and more cardinals have begun to move to the Central Grand Church in order to curry favor with the Pope #unfinished_plot_thread #privated %%


### Archbishop of the Grand Church of Science

Leaders of established churches. They can only be superseded if they are in the same church as a Cardinal. They are responsible for overseeing the churches efforts in a smaller area than the cardinals and are supposed to work to achieve their cardinals vision, which in turn matches the overall efforts of the church. However, archbishops can sometimes be overly ambitious as they can be elevated to Cardinal by the pope. 
The Archbishop is often the highest position of the church that an average scientist could have the chance to meet. They are rarely seen outside of particular religious events where they are officiating. A direct command from the Archbishop is law. While the two positions higher can obviously override an archbishops command, this will basically never happen as the higher positions would never stoop down to the minutia of giving orders or tasks to the church class.

## Special Class
### Sanctified Maiden of the Grand Church of Science

A part of the Special Class of scientist that is outside the standard hierarchy of the [[Grand Church of Science]]. They are of critical importance as the lynchpin in one of the holiest rituals of the Church, the [[Sacrament of Science]]. The Sanctified Maidens enjoy a position of reverence that could eventually even equal to the Archbishop. However, this does not mean that they have the same amount of power. Indeed, Sanctified Maidens are usually managed by the Deacon who will specifically pick out potential maidens during recruitment and ensure they are given the requisite attention and information the holy rank requires. 
A Sanctified Maiden's level of reverence depends on how their holy meeting with Science went. If they were accepted and [[Kissed by Science]] then they are highly revered for the remainder of their tenure on the mortal coil before going to join science. However, if they were instead spurned by science, it is a great shame and they are of less note than a Deacon (yet still above the Worker class and the other church classes.)

### Scalpels of the Grand Church of Science

Highly trained and specialized agents of science. Training of these operatives is the true reason for the [[Tribulations]]. Masters of espionage and  assassination, this secretive order is due no small amount of credit for the Church's reputation for quickly ingraining and asserting itself into towns. They are at the direct call of the Deacons, although their orders could be superseded by anyone with more power than one. Their authority can never be lower than a Deacons, but could be higher depending on who assigned them to a particular task. They are the secret pride of the church yet their names are whispered by the lower classes with a healthy dose of fear. It is rumored that the best of the Scalpels are in the direct employ of the Pope...

## Church Class
### Deacon of the Grand Church of Science

A highly important and respected position amongst the Church. The Deacons are responsible for the daily happenings of the church and the direct problems that arise . They are responsible for the designing and overseeing of the [[Tribulations]] as well as allocating resources and organizing quests to recover pieces of science. There are usually at least 2 deacons per parish of science. This is the highest position a scientist may expect to see regularly.
While it is not always the case, many Parishes have a Deacon responsible for the religious duties and another responsible for the political struggles the church must undergo.


### Instructors of the Grand Church of Science

A very important position that is responsible for the training of new acolyte scientists as well as ensuring logistics for science retrieval quests. They are also in charge of daily clerical duties and specific tasks around their parish building (such as head librarian, head of conversions, etc.). This role is almost always comprised of more experienced convertors who have retired from the field after completing their area missions, but on occasion acolytes are groomed for the position.

### Enforcers of the Grand Church of Science

Another very important position, Enforcers are responsible for the safety of the church and those within it. They are mostly made up of Searchers who have successfully completed a set number of science retrieval quests. Their martial prowess is passed onto acolytes and the worker class through trainings for retrieval quests. Aside from guard duties they also fill important roles such as Quartermaster, Head Armorer, etc.

## Worker Class

### Searchers of the Grand Church of Science

Part of the core classes that allow the church to function. Searchers are skilled individuals identified in the [[Tribulations]] to be good candidates for retrieval of pieces of the body of science from known or highly likely locations. It is the most coveted of the Worker positions by most acolytes.

### Convertors of the Grand Church of Science

Equal in importance yet less coveted by the masses, the Convertors of the Grand Church of Science Engage in theological warfare with opposing religions in the area or simply go out to bring potential scientists to the fold. If successful, the largely cushy and important promotion of Instructor is enticing. Convertors can go on Science Retrieval missions if they volunteer.

### Seekers of the Grand Church of Science

Least desirable and often ridiculed, to be a Seeker indicated poor performance in the [[Tribulations]]. Largely set to guarding Convertors, Seekers head out into the world to find pieces of the body of science. Given the lack of any resources allocated to this, they are usually unsuccessful. However, discovering a true piece of the body of science could promote their station to Searcher although this is a very rare occurrence, if it ever has truly happened. 


### Acolytes of the Grand Church of Science

Fresh recruits to the church who have yet to prove themselves in the tribulations, Acolytes are given grunt work of cleaning or organizing things interspersed with lessons of the church and activities to improve their skills for potential quests.

## Blasphemers

### Unbelievers

Those that do not believe in Science. Some are simply lost and it is the duty of the Convertors to guide them into the fold. They are not respected but can be conversed with.

### Other-Believers 

Those that believe in one of the heretical false gods. they were the ones who bought about the calamity of the [[The First Descent of Science]] and are the worst of humanity. They must change their ways or be eradicated. Derided, ridiculed, lambasted and damned.
